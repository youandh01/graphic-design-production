# Media Templates

Pick the closest medium. Ask only the questions that affect production.

## SNS Graphic

Ask:

- Which platform or placement?
- Is the priority announcement, reach, branding, event info, or conversion?
- Should text be inside the graphic or only in the caption?

Notes:

- Keep text readable at mobile size.
- Leave safe space near platform UI.
- Use sRGB for typical digital delivery.
- Prepare variants only after aspect ratio is confirmed.

## Poster Or Flyer

Ask:

- Is it print, digital, or both?
- What trim size and orientation are required?
- What text is mandatory?

Notes:

- Separate title, key visual, details, and call to action.
- Confirm bleed and printer requirements before final PDF.
- Do not assume CMYK, bleed, or PDF/X settings without printer guidance.

## Banner Or Signage

Ask:

- Where will it be viewed?
- What is the physical or pixel size?
- What is the typical viewing distance?

Notes:

- Use fewer words than a poster.
- Prioritize contrast and legibility.
- Confirm installation constraints for physical signage.

## Thumbnail

Ask:

- Which platform and aspect ratio?
- What is the main click reason?
- Is there required text?

Notes:

- Use one clear focal point.
- Keep text short and large.
- Test readability at small size.

## Character Or Illustration

Ask:

- Is this for campaign, mascot, editorial, merchandise, story, or icon use?
- What style level: cute, flat, semi-real, caricature, 3D, hand-drawn?
- What pose, expression, outfit, and background are confirmed?
- Is reuse needed across multiple poses or scenes?

Notes:

- Do not invent age, gender, ethnicity, clothing, or body details.
- For caricature, ask exaggeration level.
- If the character will be reused, recommend consistent proportions, palette, and layer organization.

## Brand Graphic

Ask:

- Is there an existing brand guide?
- Are logo, color, font, and spacing rules provided?
- Is this a new concept or production adaptation?

Notes:

- Do not invent brand colors or logo rules.
- Track confirmed brand assets separately from proposed options.
