# Representation Risk Checks

Use only when people, communities, occupations, regions, culture, age, gender, body/appearance, religion, public issues, accessibility, or sensitive social context appears.

## Main Rule

Represent subjects as specific, whole people or clear design elements, not as stereotypes. Do not add identity, appearance, cultural, regional, or social details the user did not provide.

## Checks

- Confirm who is represented and why.
- Confirm whether the tone is neutral, warm, humorous, formal, campaign-like, editorial, premium, or critical.
- Check whether the concept relies on stereotypes, pity, shock, exoticism, fear, ridicule, or tokenism.
- Ask before adding symbols tied to nation, religion, disability, political groups, race, ethnicity, class, occupation, or region.
- For public campaigns, ask who the campaign speaks to and what action or feeling it should create.

## Caricature

Ask what kind:

- friendly character illustration
- cute simplified portrait
- light exaggeration of expression
- editorial exaggeration
- humorous parody

Do not exaggerate traits tied to stigma, body difference, ethnicity, disability, age, or religion.
